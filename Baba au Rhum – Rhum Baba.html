BABA AU RHUM

This dessert drenched in a rum syrup and served alongside a luscious portion of Chantilly cream has remained in fashion since being first consumed by Parisians in the early 18th century. The original Parisian pastry shop, Stohrer, where French pastry chef Nicolas Stohrer first sold his famous baba au rhum in 1730 still stands

As delicate and good as it is, the baba au rhum is a far cry from the most elegant dessert out there for the French.

RECIPE

serving: 10 prep time: 30 minutes cook time: 1 hour
total time: 1 hour and 30 minutes

INGREDIENTS

FOR THE DOUGH:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 1/4 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup warm milk
  • 6 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened

FOR THE RUM SYRUP:

  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 1/2 cups water
  • 1/2 cup dark rum
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

OPTIONAL GARNISHES:

  • Fresh whipped cream
  • Candied fruits or zest
  • Additional rum for drizzling

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. In a small bowl, mix the warm milk with yeast. Let it sit for 5 minutes until foamy.
  2. In the bowl of a stand mixer, combine flour, sugar, and salt.
  3. Add eggs and the yeast mixture, then knead using the dough hook until a sticky dough forms.
  4. Gradually incorporate the butter, one tablespoon at a time, ensuring each piece is fully mixed in before adding the next.
  5. Cover the bowl with a clean towel and let the dough rise in a warm spot for 1 hour or until doubled in size.
  6. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  7. Lightly grease the molds and fill each about halfway with the dough. Now let the dough rise in the molds for 30 minutes.
  8. Bake for 15-20 minutes or until golden brown. Carefully take the cakes out of the molds and let them rest on a wire rack to cool completely.
  9. In a saucepan, combine sugar and water. Heat gently until the sugar dissolves.
  10. Take the mixture off the heat and mix in the rum and vanilla extract.
  11. Using a pastry brush or by dipping, soak each Baba thoroughly with the rum syrup. And let the Babas absorb the syrup until moist but not soggy.
  12. Serve with any of the desired garnishes.
